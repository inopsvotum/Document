# Home Core Training Guide (Bench + Dumbbells Only)

## Goal
Build a stronger, tighter core at home with minimal equipment, while supporting fat-loss training.

> Core training improves posture and abdominal control. Fat loss from diet/activity is still the main driver of belly-fat reduction.

---

## Equipment
- Flat bench
- 1–2 dumbbells
- Mat/towel (optional)

---

## Warm-up (3–5 min)
1. Cat-cow x 6 reps
2. Hip circles x 10/side
3. Bodyweight glute bridge x 10
4. Slow dead-bug pattern x 4/side

---

## Main Exercises (No Machines)

## 1) Dead Bug
**Why:** Deep core control, protects lower back.

**How to do it**
1. Lie on your back, arms up, knees/hips at 90°.
2. Brace your abs and gently press lower back toward floor.
3. Extend opposite arm + leg slowly.
4. Return and switch sides.

**Sets/Reps:** 3 x 8–12 per side  
**Tempo:** slow, controlled  
**Common mistakes:** lower back arching, rushing reps.

**Reference image:**  
- https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Dead_Bug_Exercise.png *(Wikimedia Commons)*

---

## 2) Bench Reverse Crunch
**Why:** Lower-ab and pelvic control focus.

**How to do it**
1. Lie on bench, hold bench edge behind your head.
2. Start with knees bent.
3. Bring knees toward chest and curl pelvis slightly off bench.
4. Lower slowly (don’t swing legs).

**Sets/Reps:** 3 x 10–15  
**Common mistakes:** using momentum, dropping legs too fast.

**Reference image:**  
- https://www.bodybuilding.com/images/2020/xdb/cropped/xdb-70m-bench-reverse-crunch-m1-square-600x600.jpg *(Bodybuilding.com exercise library)*

---

## 3) Dumbbell Russian Twist
**Why:** Obliques and rotational control.

**How to do it**
1. Sit on floor, lean torso slightly back, chest up.
2. Hold one dumbbell at chest.
3. Rotate left/right with control.
4. Keep ribs down; avoid rounding lower back.

**Sets/Reps:** 3 x 16–24 total reps  
**Common mistakes:** swinging too fast, only moving arms (not torso).

**Reference image:**  
- https://www.bodybuilding.com/images/2020/xdb/cropped/xdb-82s-russian-twist-m1-square-600x600.jpg *(Bodybuilding.com exercise library)*

---

## 4) Dumbbell Side Bend
**Why:** Oblique strength and lateral trunk stability.

**How to do it**
1. Stand tall, dumbbell in one hand, other hand behind head/at side.
2. Bend slowly toward weighted side.
3. Return to neutral (don’t overextend opposite side).
4. Complete reps, then switch sides.

**Sets/Reps:** 3 x 10–15 per side  
**Common mistakes:** twisting instead of side bending, going too heavy.

**Reference image:**  
- https://www.bodybuilding.com/images/2020/xdb/cropped/xdb-61d-one-arm-dumbbell-side-bend-m1-square-600x600.jpg *(Bodybuilding.com exercise library)*

## Weekly Integration (Simple)

## Option A: 2 days/week (minimum effective)
- **Tue:** Dead Bug + Reverse Crunch
- **Fri:** Russian Twist + Side Bend

## Option B: 3 days/week (better)
- **Mon:** Dead Bug + Reverse Crunch
- **Wed:** Russian Twist + Side Bend
- **Sat:** Dead Bug + Side Bend

**Session length:** 10–15 minutes  
**Best timing:** after your workout or later at home on non-heavy days.

---

## Progression Plan (4 weeks)

## Week 1
- Start at lower reps with perfect form.

## Week 2
- Add 1–2 reps per set.

## Week 3
- Add slight dumbbell load (or 1 extra set on one exercise).

## Week 4
- Keep load, improve control/tempo (2 sec up, 2 sec down).

Then repeat cycle.

**Progress rule:** increase only one variable at a time (reps, load, or sets).

---

## Safety & Form Checklist
- Brace before every rep (“tight stomach”).
- Keep lower back neutral, avoid excessive arching.
- Stop if sharp pain (especially low back/hip flexor pinch).
- Use controlled tempo; no jerking/swinging.
- Leave 1–3 reps in reserve (don’t grind to failure on core).
